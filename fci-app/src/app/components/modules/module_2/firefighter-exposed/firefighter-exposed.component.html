What Are Firefighters Exposed To?

Module 2

Firefighters are exposed to toxins considered either known carcinogens or probable carcinogens, such as:

Polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons (PAHs)

Examples: Benzo[a]pyrene, Napthalene, Acenapthylene

Volatile Organic Compounds (VOCs)

Examples: Formaldehyde, Benzene

Asbestos
Diesel

Where are Firefighters Exposed?

  Module 2

Inhalation

Toxins can be inhaled on the scene, at the station, and en route.

Absorption

Toxins can be absorbed through the skin. Increases in temperature increases skins ability to absorb. The face, jaw, neck, and throat are more susceptible.

Ingestion

Toxins can be accidentally ingested when drinking or eating without first completing proper decontamination.
