  Module 3

Wear PPE and SCBA during all phases of firefighting and overhaul

Set up rehab in cold zone away from dirty gear

Complete post fire on scene gross decon

Clean exposed areas with wipes before eating/drinking or after handling contaminated gear

  • Hands, neck, face and head
  • Do not cross contaminate across body parts
  • Use additional wipes as necessary

Implement on scene hood and glove swap program

Reducing Risk: At the Station

  Module 3

Diesel Exhaust Reduction

  • Open garage doors before starting vehicles
  • Don’t run vehicles in closed bays
  • If possible, keep vehicle 15 – 20 feet away from bay when running
  • Reduce time vehicle operates in bay area
  • If possible, keep bay doors open at least 10 minutes after a vehicle runs in bay
  • Keep doors between the garage and other areas of the fire house closed
  • Store gear away from engines in a separate ventilated room
  • Implement protocols to reduce spread of contaminants at the Station

Reducing Risk: Clean Cab Concept

  Module 3

  • Place firefighter equipment (SCBA, irons, etc) in outside compartments
  • Use materials inside/out easy to decontaminate
  • Light color interior, non-absorbent materials, vinyl seats not fabric
A clean cab apparatus is an apparatus designed to facilitate a clean, healthy, and safe environment by reducing the exposure to contaminates associated with occupational exposures found in firefighting activities.

  Module 3

  • Smooth Surfaces

Reducing Risks: Organizational Level

  Module 4

Firefighter Cancer Initiative FAST-C Survey Findings

  • Surveyed over 100 Florida firefighters
  • Department size, budget, makeup (volunteer vs career), or location (urban vs rural) do not drive success
  • Factors that support fire departments offering cancer screening activities:
    • Written policy
    • Dedicated occupational health and safety program
    • Dedicated Health and Safety Officers
