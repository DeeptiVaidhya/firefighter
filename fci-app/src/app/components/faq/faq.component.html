What is FCI?

The FCI Â program is an online tool for helping HIV+ cancer survivors adapt to the daily challenges of co-managing these two chronic conditions. In particular, we hope that FCI addresses specific needs of gay and bisexual men and trans women, which are commonly overlooked in available resources for people living with HIV who are also cancer survivors.

What is the purpose of FCI?

Based on nearly three decades of research, FCI brings together the knowledge of experts at Northwestern University and the University of Miami. Members of our team have studied a wide range of topics, including the psychosocial aspects of disease management, health promotion in the LGBT population, and the use of technology to improve health outcomes. We hope that sharing our expertise will help you to cope more effectively with cancer and HIV.

How can the FCI study help me?

By participating in this study, you will have access to all of the information included in the FCI website. Many of the resources in FCI may be relevant to your experience living with both HIV and cancer, as well as daily challenges and stressors.

Who has access to FCI?

Only enrolled participants and approved study staff have access to the FCI website.
