Firefighter Cancer Initiative:

The Firefighter Cancer Initiative (FCI) was launched in 2015 through a state of Florida appropriation. The primary goals of FCI are to better document and understand the excess burden of cancer among Florida firefighters, and identify novel, evidence-based methods for reducing risk. The initiative is led by a multidisciplinary team of scientists, healthcare practitioners and occupational health and safety experts and uses community engaged approaches to ensure that firefightersâ€™ voice and occupational experience is reflected throughout all aspects of program planning and implementation.

FCI Firefighter Cancer Prevention Curriculum (FCPC):

The FCI Cancer Prevention Curriculum, created in partnership with fire service leaders, aims to educate firefighters on cancer risk associated with the fire service and how to reduce these risks at the individual and organizational levels. Additional learning resources with state and national level fire organizations are shared for reference. he curriculum is comprised of 5 modules, 1) Cancer in the fire service; 2) Firefighter Occupational Cancer Risk; 3) Reducing Cancer Risk in the Fire Service; 4) Organizational Change and 5) Ongoing Learning Resources. Each module consists of the most up to date recommendations for firefighters with videos, interactive activities and short quizzes for evaluation.

FCI Cancer Survivorship Program:

The FCI Cancer Survivorship program aims to build a better understanding of what cancer survivorship is, the expected physical and emotional changes and how to prepare for the many changes that occur during diagnosis, treatment and returning to the fire service. This online portal has been tailor made for firefighters and based on experiences of firefighter cancer survivors.
